### 1. [HIGH] Inventory Aging — Atlanta Entry Level

**Market:** Atlanta | **Segment:** Entry Level | **Owner:** market-ops | **Confidence:** Medium-High

#### Summary
Atlanta entry level inventory is aging materially, with 5.8% of active listings (14 homes) exceeding 90 days on market and average DOM now at 52.9 days. The trend has been worsening for multiple consecutive weeks, indicating a structural demand-supply imbalance rather than a temporary seasonal blip. Without intervention, these homes will require markdown pricing to clear, compressing resale margins from current 6.2%.

#### Evidence
- Cumulative DOM increase vs 4-week baseline: +31.3 days (21.6 → 52.9), confirming a sustained deterioration trend.
- Current average DOM is 52.9 days for Atlanta entry level. Sustained DOM growth signals reduced buyer demand or overpriced resale listings.
- Aged inventory typically requires price reductions of 3-8% to clear, directly compressing resale margins.

#### Root Causes
- Resale pricing too aggressive: Homes listed above the market's current buyer demand level in Atlanta are not moving at list price. The progressive DOM increase over 6+ weeks suggests initial listing prices were set based on acquisition comps rather than current resale demand.
- Demand softening in Atlanta: Broader market conditions may have weakened since acquisition — rising mortgage rates, seasonal slowdown, or increased competing inventory. The entry level segment appears most exposed, possibly due to buyer affordability constraints at this price tier.
- Insufficient markdown cadence: Opendoor's standard markdown trigger (typically at 30/60/90 days) may not be responding fast enough to the Atlanta market velocity, allowing homes to accumulate in the 90+ day bucket faster than they are cleared.

#### Business Impact
With 14 homes in the 90+ day bucket at 5.8% of inventory, Opendoor faces a choice between markdown pricing (estimated 3-8% reduction to clear) or continued carrying costs (~$1,200-2,500/month/home for Atlanta). At current resale margins of 6.2%, a 5% markdown would eliminate the margin entirely on aging homes. Capital tied up in aged inventory also reduces Opendoor's capacity to acquire new homes in more favorable segments.

#### Recommended Actions
1. Market-ops team: Implement an immediate 3% price reduction on all Atlanta entry level listings exceeding 75 days DOM. For homes over 100 days, apply a 5% reduction. Execute price changes by end of business Friday.
2. Pricing team: Review acquisition offers in Atlanta entry level for the past 8 weeks and identify if any AVM segments or zip codes show systematic overvaluation at resale. Pause new acquisitions in zip codes with 3+ aged homes until the resale velocity audit is complete.
