## 3. TOP SIGNALS TO ACT ON

### Signal 1: Charlotte Sales Volume Collapse (−17.1% MoM, −43% vs. avg)
Only 580 homes sold vs. a historical average of ~1,018. Combined with the highest months of supply (5.0), this market is rapidly losing liquidity. **Any asset we hold here is harder to exit.**

### Signal 2: Atlanta Price Decline Accelerating (−2.5% MoM — steepest portfolio-wide)
Despite a lower risk score than Charlotte, Atlanta is seeing the fastest price erosion. At this rate, that's a ~30% annualized decline. Likely overshooting, but the direction is clear and accelerating.

### Signal 3: DOM Anomaly Across All Markets (1.7–1.8 standard deviations above average)
Every single market has DOM well above historical norms — Phoenix worst at 50 days (avg: 38). Properties are sitting. This universality suggests a macro factor (rates, sentiment) rather than local issues.

## 4. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS THIS WEEK

1. **Charlotte — PAUSE all new acquisitions.** Risk score 9 with deteriorating fundamentals on every metric. Re-evaluate pipeline deals with a minimum 8–10% discount to current comps to account for continued price declines.

2. **Atlanta — Stress-test the current portfolio.** Apply a −5% to −10% price correction scenario to all Atlanta holdings. Identify any assets with thin margins that should be accelerated to disposition before further softening.

3. **Dallas & Phoenix — Shift to opportunistic-only.** These markets are softening but not broken. Approve acquisitions only at ≥6% discount to 90-day comps, and only for assets with strong rent-yield fundamentals that don't depend on near-term appreciation.

4. **All Markets — Renegotiate any deals under contract.** With DOM surging and sellers losing leverage across the board, there is justification to request price adjustments or concessions on any pending acquisitions.
